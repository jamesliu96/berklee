Audio Levels

Also complicating matters when connecting devices, are the different levels used for various devices.

Mixers generally display signal level using VU meters or peak level LEDs. These meters show optimal signal level as 0dBu.

Electronic equipment accepts input signals that are at what we refer to as line level. There are two standards for this. Most consumer or semi-pro electronic instruments are characterized as 'line' or 'instrument' level and generally use unbalanced RCA phono or 1/4" phone connectors. These signals show up at 0dBu on VU meters when the electrical signal level is at -10dBV.

For most professional studio devices, line level is +4dBV. These connections typically use balanced XLR connectors and show as 0dBu on VU meters when their electrical signal level is at +4dBV. The owner's manual will state the level and plug/jack configurations for all device inputs and outputs. If you need to connect devices with differing signal level requirements, you can purchase specific transformers for going from -10dB to +4dB or vice-versa.

Microphone levels are generally in the -40 to -30 dBV range. For this reason, any time you record with a microphone and an audio interface, you'll need to use some sort of preamp that will boost the signal from the microphone to line level. Both the MOTU 828mkII and the Pro Tools M-Box have built-in microphone preamps.

One advantage to working in the virtual studio is that until you need to connect your personal computer to a sound system, you never need worry about signal level mismatch.

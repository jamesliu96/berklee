How Loud Is It?

The following table gives some general references for loudness.

Remember, as musicians we think of loudness in a musical performance in subjective terms we call dynamic markings. The table below indicates how the objective measurement of sound pressure levels in dB (decibels) relates to sonic events in our daily lives as well as musical dynamics performed by a symphony orchestra.

 

Decibels Sonic Event Dynamic (Orchestra)
0 dB Threshold of hearing
10 dB Pin dropping
20 dB Whisper
30 dB Quiet conversation
40 dB Normal Conversation ppp
50 dB Noisy office pp
60 dB Busy Street (Mass. Ave., noon) p
70 dB Factory workshop mf
80 dB Expressway f
90 dB Loud Truck ff
100 dB Power tool at close range fff
110 dB Loud rock music
120 dB Jet taking off
130 dB Threshold of pain

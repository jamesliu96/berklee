Recording Tools

Here are some types of tools commonly used to record musical performances in a desktop music production system.

Type of Tool

Example

MDM-Modular Digital Multitrack - An 8-track digital tape recorder that records onto either VHS or Hi-8 video video tape formats. Multiple units from the same manufacturer can be linked to provide 16, 24, 32 and in the case of Tascam, up to 128 tracks.

Pictured here: Alesis ADAT M-20

Hard-Disk Recorder - With the success of computer based systems, manufacturers have begun to produce self-contained hard-disk recorders. These typically are capable of recording 24 tracks, and provide basic editing features as well as access to a computer interface.

Pictured here: Tascam HDR2496

DAT Recorder - Originally concieved as a consumer format, the digital audio tape recorder is a very common type of stereo mixdown deck. A DAT can record two tracks on its own tape format.

Pictured here: Tascam DA-20

MiniDisk Recorder - Another common consumer format, the MiniDisk is used as a mixdown deck in many home studios. These devices record two tracks on their own type of optical disk. Since a MiniDisc uses a form of data compression, it's not considered to be a professional format.

Pictured here: Sony Mindisk

CD Recorder -Several manufacturers produce devices that can record directly to CD. Although we usually want to edit and sequence audio files before making a CD, these devices can be very useful in making demo mixes.

Pictured here: Sony CDRW66

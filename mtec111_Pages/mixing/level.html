Audio Levels

When connecting audio devices, there are the different levels used for various devices.

Mixers generally display signal level using VU meters or peak level LEDs. These meters show optimal signal level as 0dBu.

Most consumer or semi-pro electronic instruments are characterized as 'line' or 'instrument' level and generally use unbalanced RCA phono or 1/4" phone connectors. Line level signals show up at 0dBu on VU meters when the electrical signal level is at -10dBV.

Professional recording devices typically use balanced XLR connectors and show as 0dBu on VU meters when their electrical signal level is +4dBV. The owner's manuals will state the level and plug/jack configurations for all device inputs and outputs. If you need to connect devices with differing signal level requirements, you can purchase specific transformers for going from -10dB to +4dB or vice-versa.

Microphone levels are generally in the -40 to -30 dBV range.

One advantage to working in the virtual studio is that until you need to connect your personal computer to a sound system, you never need worry about signal level mismatch.

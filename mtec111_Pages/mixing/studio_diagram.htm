Signal Flow In A Typical Production Environment

 
In a typical record/mixdown session the control room is usually acoustically isolated from the recording room. The following signal routing needs to be accomplished:
  • Signal from a microphone in the recording room needs to be routed to the multitrack record deck.
  • The performer in headphones needs to monitor previously recorded tracks coming from the multitrack record deck.
  • The performer needs to hear themselves (the output of their microphone) clearly in the headphones.
  • An engineer in the control room needs to monitor playback from the multitrack record deck as well as the performer.
  • The output of the multitrack record deck needs to be routed to the mixing board, effects processors and finally to a stereo mixdown deck.
